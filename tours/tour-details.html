Tour Description

Embark on an unforgettable journey through Lisbon's most historic neighborhood, Alfama. This walking tour will take you through narrow cobblestone streets, past colorful houses adorned with traditional Portuguese tiles, and up to breathtaking viewpoints that offer panoramic views of the city and the Tagus River. During our 3-hour exploration, you'll discover the authentic soul of Lisbon as we visit traditional Fado houses where Portugal's national music was born. Learn about the fascinating history of this UNESCO World Heritage music genre and perhaps catch a glimpse of spontaneous performances by local musicians. We'll explore the magnificent Lisbon Cathedral, one of the city's oldest and most important religious buildings, and wander through the maze-like streets that have remained virtually unchanged for centuries. You'll hear stories of Moorish occupation, medieval times, and the golden age of Portuguese discoveries. The tour includes visits to several miradouros (viewpoints) including Miradouro da Senhora do Monte and Miradouro das Portas do Sol, where you'll capture Instagram-worthy photos and truly appreciate why Lisbon is called the city of seven hills. This intimate small-group experience (maximum 12 people) ensures personal attention from your knowledgeable local guide, who will share insider tips about the best local restaurants, hidden gems, and cultural insights that you won't find in guidebooks.
